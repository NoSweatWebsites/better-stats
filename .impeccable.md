## Design Context

### Users
BetterStats is used by an internal ops team to monitor AI visibility, and the outputs are also shared with clients. The core job is to quickly understand “are we showing up?”, “where/why not?”, and “what changed over time?” across multiple AI models—without noise.

### Brand Personality
- Analytical, calm, minimal
- Data-forward and trustworthy; clarity over decoration
- Confident, understated polish (the product should feel “quietly premium”)

### Aesthetic Direction
- Light mode only.
- Benchmark the overall feel on `sitechecker.pro`: clean layout, crisp typography, soft borders, restrained color, high information density with strong hierarchy.
- Use a predominantly neutral foundation (white/near-white surfaces, subtle gray dividers) and reserve brand colors for emphasis, state, and key data highlights.
- Brand palette (use intentionally, not everywhere):
  - #A7EF48 (lime)
  - #A654FD (purple)
  - #E6E0C4 (sand)
  - #FD3C16 (red)
  - #14342B (deep green)
  - #0B201F (near-black green)

### Design Principles
1. Calm data density: show lots of information, but with whitespace, grouping, and typography doing the work.
2. Color is a signal: neutrals first; brand colors only to encode meaning (primary actions, selected states, key metrics, alerts).
3. Consistent surfaces: cards, tables, and charts share the same radius, border weight, shadow, and padding scale.
4. Fast scanning over reading: prioritize summaries, deltas, badges, and tooltips; make “what matters” visible in 3 seconds.
5. Client-ready by default: anything shareable should look polished, neutral, and presentation-safe (no dev-y artifacts).
