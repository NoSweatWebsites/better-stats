# SEO Keyword Research Without Expensive Tools

Use this skill when the user wants to find SEO content topics, generate keyword ideas, prioritize articles, or build a lightweight organic content plan using free or low-cost research methods.

This skill is especially useful for:

- Early-stage websites without access to paid SEO suites
- Content strategy projects that need topic clusters
- Blog, pillar-page, or content-calendar planning
- Finding quick-win keywords from search, SERPs, and communities
- Prioritizing content by demand, competition, intent, and opportunity

## Required Inputs

Before starting, collect these inputs from the user. If some are missing, ask only for the missing details that are essential to proceed.

1. **Niche or industry**: What space is the site, brand, or business in?
2. **Target audience**: Who is the content for?
3. **Business goal**: Is the priority traffic, leads, sales, authority, education, or something else?
4. **Existing content**: Does the user already have a site, blog, content library, or topic focus?
5. **Geography or market**: Which country, region, or language should the keyword research target?
6. **Site maturity**: New, growing, or established.

If the user cannot provide all inputs, proceed with explicit assumptions.

## Core Workflow

Follow this workflow in order unless the user asks for a narrower output.

### 1. Clarify the SEO Context

Summarize the working assumptions:

- Niche
- Audience
- Business goal
- Market or geography
- Site maturity
- Existing content status

Then decide whether the content strategy should lean toward:

- Informational content for awareness
- Commercial investigation content for comparison and evaluation
- Transactional or bottom-of-funnel content for conversion
- Authority-building pillar content
- Community-led pain-point content

## 2. Generate Seed Keywords

Create an initial set of 50 to 100 seed keyword ideas using free research routes.

Use these sources and patterns:

### Google Autocomplete Patterns

Generate variants such as:

- `[keyword] + a-z variations`
- `how to [keyword]`
- `best [keyword] for [audience/use case]`
- `[keyword] vs [alternative]`
- `[keyword] cost`
- `[keyword] examples`
- `[keyword] template`
- `[keyword] strategy`
- `[keyword] mistakes`

### People Also Ask Patterns

Capture questions related to:

- Problems
- Definitions
- Comparisons
- Costs
- Alternatives
- How-to tasks
- Risks
- Best practices

### Community Mining Sources

Use community sources to identify how real people describe the problem:

- Reddit
- Quora
- Industry forums
- YouTube comments
- Amazon reviews, where relevant
- Niche communities or specialist publications

Extract:

- Repeated questions
- Pain points
- Objections
- Desired outcomes
- Confusing terminology
- Existing solution complaints
- Phrases and language the audience actually uses

## 3. Apply the 6 Circles Method

Use one primary keyword to create a 13-part topic structure:

- 1 primary keyword or pillar topic
- 3 supporting sub-topics
- 9 specific content pieces, with 3 articles under each sub-topic

### Structure

```text
Primary keyword or pillar topic
├── Sub-topic A
│   ├── Article 1
│   ├── Article 2
│   └── Article 3
├── Sub-topic B
│   ├── Article 4
│   ├── Article 5
│   └── Article 6
└── Sub-topic C
    ├── Article 7
    ├── Article 8
    └── Article 9
```

### How to Choose the Primary Keyword

Choose a primary keyword that is:

- Central to the niche
- Commercially or strategically relevant
- Broad enough to support a pillar page
- Specific enough to reflect search intent
- Capable of supporting multiple sub-topics

### How to Choose Sub-topics

Each sub-topic should represent a meaningful supporting theme. Good sub-topics often map to:

- Audience problems
- Use cases
- Buying criteria
- Implementation steps
- Comparisons
- Common misconceptions
- Beginner education
- Advanced strategy

## 4. Use Varied Content Types

For the 9 supporting articles, vary formats based on search intent.

Use these content types where appropriate:

| Content type | Pattern |
|---|---|
| How-to guide | `How to [achieve outcome] with [method]` |
| Comparison | `[Option A] vs [Option B]: which is better for [use case]` |
| List post | `[Number] [adjective] ways to [achieve result]` |
| Case study | `How [persona] achieved [result] using [approach]` |
| Trend analysis | `[Topic] trends in [year]: what is changing` |
| Expert roundup | `[Number] experts share their [topic] strategies` |
| Beginner guide | `[Topic] for beginners: everything you need to know` |
| Tool review | `Best [category] tools for [audience/use case]` |
| Problem-solution | `Why [problem happens] and how to fix it` |

Match the format to the likely search intent rather than forcing variety for its own sake.

## 6. Run Manual SERP Competition Assessment

For each target keyword, assess the top 10 organic results manually.

Score opportunity by looking for these signals:

| Signal | What to inspect | Opportunity exists when |
|---|---|---|
| Content depth | Word count, detail, comprehensiveness | Top results are thin or generic |
| Freshness | Publication or update date | Results are outdated or pre-date major changes |
| Format match | Whether ranking pages match search intent | The SERP format does not satisfy the query |
| Authority gap | Strength of ranking domains | Smaller or niche sites are already ranking |
| Content gaps | Missing subtopics or unanswered questions | PAA questions or obvious concerns are not covered |
| Specificity | Whether content is tailored to the audience | Results are broad and not audience-specific |

Do not promise rankings. Treat this as an opportunity assessment, not a guarantee.

## 7. Prioritize Keywords

Score each keyword from 1 to 10 across:

- **Demand**: Estimated search demand or demonstrated audience interest
- **Competition**: Difficulty, where lower means easier
- **Intent fit**: How closely the query matches the user's business goal
- **Opportunity**: Demand minus competition, adjusted by content gaps and intent

Use this prioritization rule:

- Prioritize keywords with opportunity score above 3
- Prioritize keywords with intent fit above 6
- For new sites, prefer lower volume and lower competition
- For established sites, include broader and more competitive terms when strategically important

### Sweet Spot Targets

| Site stage | Monthly search volume target | Competition level |
|---|---:|---|
| New site, 0 to 6 months | 100 to 500 | Low |
| Growing site, 6 to 18 months | 500 to 2,000 | Low to medium |
| Established site, 18+ months | 1,000 to 10,000 | Medium |

## 8. Reddit Mining Protocol

Use these search patterns when community insight would improve the strategy:

```text
site:reddit.com "[niche]" "how do I"
site:reddit.com "[niche]" "help"
site:reddit.com "[niche]" "struggling with"
site:reddit.com "[niche]" "recommend"
site:reddit.com "[niche]" "vs"
```

Extract:

- Repeated questions as content opportunities
- Complaints about existing solutions as differentiation angles
- Terminology and slang as keyword variations
- Recommended resources as competitor references
- Upvote and comment patterns as demand signals

## Quality Rules

- Always connect keyword choices to the user's business goal.
- Do not chase volume alone. Prioritize intent and realistic ranking opportunity.
- Be explicit when volume, difficulty, or competition data is estimated rather than verified.
- Use audience language from communities wherever possible.
- Distinguish between informational, commercial, and transactional intent.
- Flag content gaps that can create a stronger angle than existing results.
- For new sites, bias toward long-tail, low-competition, high-intent topics.
- For established sites, include pillar topics and competitive terms when they support authority.
- Never guarantee rankings, traffic, leads, or sales.
- If exact keyword metrics are unavailable, provide directional ranges and explain how the user can validate them in Google Keyword Planner or another free tool.

## What This Skill Does Not Do

This skill does not:

- Provide guaranteed exact search volumes
- Replace a full technical SEO audit
- Analyze existing site performance without Search Console or analytics data
- Guarantee rankings
- Replace paid SEO suites for advanced competitive research
- Produce full article drafts unless the user asks for writing support

- Write the first article or pillar page
- Create a brief for a writer or editor
